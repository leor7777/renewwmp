Our Story

This park
raised us.

Growing up in Tribeca, Washington Market Park was the center of everything. Every day after school, and for hours on end during the hot summer days, it was the place to be. Many of our earliest memories involve this park. Running through the sprinklers to cool off in the summer heat. Throwing a football around on the lawn. Playing tag in the playground. Watching a concert on the grass.

Washington Market Park was a place of joy for kids and parents alike. Now, we want to make sure the next generation gets to experience it the same way we did.

The Challenge

A playground
past its prime.

In recent years, the park has shown its age. The lawn suffered from drainage issues and grew muddy with dead grass. Thankfully, with help from the Friends of Washington Market Park and funding from Councilmember Marte, these drainage issues were largely fixed last summer, and the renovation repaved the paths as well.

But there is still work to be done. The playground structures are over 20 years old — well past the industry-standard lifespan of 10–15 years. The equipment shows visible rust and wear, and replacement parts may no longer be manufactured. The city has allocated $200K toward improvements, but the full scope of work is estimated at $500K or more. Renew WMP exists to bridge that gap.

An accessibility problem.
The current playground predates modern accessibility standards. According to the NYC Parks Department, the playground has no ramps, no accessible swings, no mobility transfer systems, and no companion seating for wheelchair users. Children with physical disabilities cannot access most of the play structures. A renovation isn't just about new equipment — it's about inclusion.
The Plan

How we
get there.

Through community fundraising, benefit events, and local business partnerships, Renew WMP is raising the funds needed to supplement city investment and make a full playground renovation possible.

1

Community Outreach

Connecting with Tribeca families, building boards, and the farmers market community to build awareness and support for the renovation.

2

Business Partnerships

Partnering with local Tribeca businesses for sponsorships, auction item donations, and event support.

3

Benefit Event

Hosting a fundraising benefit with auctions, direct donations, and community engagement — putting every dollar toward playground renovation.

4

Playground Renovation

Working with the NYC Parks Department and the Friends of Washington Market Park to fund and execute a playground that's safe, accessible, and built for the next 20 years.

Who We Are

Tribeca kids,
for Tribeca kids.

Renew WMP was founded by a group of Tribeca students who grew up in Washington Market Park. We played here every day — and we're not ready to watch it fall behind. This initiative is our way of giving back to the place that gave us our childhoods.
